Course Resources

All readings, videos, and materials for CSCI 247. Resources are organized by topic. Each module's index page lists specific assigned materials.

No textbook required. All materials are freely available online or provided as PDFs.

For Instructors: PDFs should be placed in the resources/pdfs/ folder. Update links below as materials are added. Verify all web links each semester.

AI Foundations

Used in: Modules 1, 2

How Large Language Models Work

Article What Are Large Language Models?

IBM Think. Clear explanation of LLM architecture, training, and limitations. Accessible for non-technical readers.

Assigned: Module 1

Video How ChatGPT Actually Works

MIT Sloan / Rama Ramakrishnan (~8 min). Visual explanation of word prediction, training data, and why LLMs make mistakes.

Assigned: Module 1

Article Optional What Is ChatGPT Doing ... and Why Does It Work?

Stephen Wolfram. Deep technical dive with visualizations. For students wanting more depth.

Testing & Reproducibility

Used in: Module 2

Article Reproducible AI: Why It Matters

AIMultiple. Only 30% of AI research is reproducible. Explains why same prompts give different results.

Assigned: Module 2

Website Calling Bullshit: Course Syllabus

Bergstrom & West, University of Washington. Critical thinking framework for evaluating claims. Selected modules assigned throughout course.

Assigned: Modules 2, 3

Data Visualization

Used in: Module 4

Identifying Misleading Graphs

Article How to Identify Misleading Graphs

ThoughtSpot. Five deceptive techniques: manipulated axes, cherry-picked data, non-uniform scales. Includes verification checklist.

Assigned: Module 4

Article Bad Data Visualization: 9 Examples

Luzmo. Real-world examples of misleading graphs with analysis of what went wrong.

Assigned: Module 4

Tool Calling Bullshit: Visualization Tools

Tools for analyzing misleading axes and proportional ink violations.

Assigned: Module 4 (optional)

AI Benchmarks

Article Is Your AI Benchmark Lying to You?

Nature. Overview of benchmark problems: data contamination, brittle performance, unsupported claims.

Assigned: Module 4

Article Weaknesses in AI Evaluation

Oxford Internet Institute. Research showing ~50% of benchmarks fail to define what they measure.

Assigned: Module 4
