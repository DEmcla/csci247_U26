Methodology Audit

Purpose: Review a classmate's work to assess whether their methodology is documented well enough to reproduce. Practice giving constructive feedback.

Time: 30-45 minutes

The Exercise

You'll review a classmate's Reproducibility Test or Prompt Sensitivity exercise from Module 2.

Step 1: Get a Submission to Review

Your instructor will assign you a classmate's submission, or you'll select one from the peer review pool.

Whose work are you reviewing?

Step 2: Attempt to Understand the Methodology

Read through their submission. Based only on what they documented:

What exactly did they test?

What tools/models did they use?

What prompts did they use (exact wording)?

What settings or conditions applied?

Step 3: Reproducibility Assessment

Could you reproduce this test exactly? (Yes/No/Partially)

What information is missing that you'd need?

What assumptions would you have to make?

Would different assumptions lead to different results?

Step 4: Constructive Feedback

Write feedback that's specific, actionable, and kind.

What did they do well?

What specific improvements would make their methodology clearer?

What questions would you ask them?

Step 5: Self-Reflection

Looking at someone else's documentation, what will you do differently in your own work?

What's harder about documenting methodology than you expected?

Peer Review Guidelines: Be specific ("I couldn't tell what version of ChatGPT you used") rather than vague ("needs more detail"). Focus on the documentation, not the person. The goal is helping each other improve.

Submission

Submit your reproducibility assessment and feedback. Your classmate will receive your feedback (anonymously or not, per instructor preference).
