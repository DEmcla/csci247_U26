Graph or Garbage

Purpose: Evaluate an AI-generated visualization for accuracy, appropriateness, and potential misleading elements.

Time: 30-45 minutes

The Exercise

Step 1: Prepare Your Data

Use this simple dataset (or create your own):

Monthly Sales Data:
January: $45,000
February: $52,000
March: $48,000
April: $61,000
May: $58,000
June: $67,000

Step 2: Ask AI to Visualize

Give the data to an AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini) and ask it to create or describe a visualization. Try a prompt like:

"Create a chart showing these monthly sales figures. What type of chart would best represent this data?"

Step 3: Evaluate the Visualization

Apply what you learned from the Excel lesson:

Is this the right chart type for the data? Why or why not?

What axis choices were made? Are they appropriate?

Does the visualization accurately represent the data?

What story does this chart tell? Is that story fair to the data?

Step 4: Identify Issues

What's misleading or problematic about the AI's visualization (if anything)?

What choices would you change?

What questions should a viewer ask before trusting this chart?

Step 5: Create Your Own Version

In Excel, create what you consider the most honest, clear representation of this data. How does it differ from the AI's version?

Key Insight: AI tools make visualization choices automatically, often without explaining why. Now that you understand how chart choices shape interpretation, you can evaluate whether AI-generated visualizations are honest or misleading.
