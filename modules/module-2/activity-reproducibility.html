Reproducibility Test

Purpose: Test whether different AI tools give consistent results for the same prompt. Learn to design and document controlled comparisons.

Time: 45-60 minutes

The Experiment

You'll ask the exact same question to three different AI tools and compare the results.

Step 1: Choose Your Prompt

Select a factual question that has a verifiable answer. Good examples:

  • "What is the population of Tokyo?"
  • "Who wrote the novel 'Beloved'?"
  • "What year was the first iPhone released?"
  • "What is the chemical formula for table salt?"

Analysis

Did all three tools give the same answer?

If not, what differed? (Content? Confidence? Level of detail?)

Could someone else reproduce your test using your documentation?

What would you do differently next time?

Why This Matters: "It worked for me" isn't evidence. Without documented methodology, you can't verify claims, compare results, or understand why something worked (or didn't). Reproducibility is the foundation of trustworthy evaluation.
